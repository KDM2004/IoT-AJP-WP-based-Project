Arduino sensor


It can sense things in the environment around it, like light, temperature, or motion. Instead of seeing these things, the sensor sends electrical signals. You can connect the sensor to an Arduino board, which is like a mini computer. The Arduino board understands the signals and can be programmed to react to them!

Resistor


A resistor is a tiny little roadblock for electricity. Imagine tiny particles, called electrons, flowing through a wire like cars on a highway. A resistor makes the path narrower, so fewer electrons can flow through at once. This is useful for controlling electricity in circuits. Resistors come in different sizes, like different sized roads, to allow more or less electricity through.

Toggle Switch


A toggle switch is like a little lever. You can move it up and down, or sometimes left and right. Each position controls something, like a light being on or off. It's like a switch with two choices! You can find them on appliances, light switches, and even on your computer.

LED bulb


An LED bulb is a type of light bulb that uses tiny lights called LEDs to create bright light. Unlike old-fashioned incandescent bulbs, LED bulbs use much less energy, which saves you money on your electricity bill. They also last for many years, so you won't need to replace them as often. LED bulbs come in all sorts of shapes and sizes, so you can find ones that fit your needs. They are a great way to light up your home and help the environment!
